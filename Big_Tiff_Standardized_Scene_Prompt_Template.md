# Big Tiff — Standardized Scene Prompt Template & Guidelines for Sammy

**Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** June 19, 2026  
**Purpose:** To ensure both versions of StoryForge Sammy create consistent, high-quality, and supportive scene prompts that align with the project’s governance, protect Erica’s creative process, and integrate smoothly with the webtool (Outline panel, review workflow, and ADHD-friendly reading experience).

---

## Before Creating Any Scene Prompt

- Always check the most recent Master Story Bible on Google Drive first.
- Confirm you are working from the current canon (especially after any changes made with the other Sammy).
- If you are unsure about any canon details, check with Erica or Aaron before proceeding.
- Remember: Erica’s drafts are sacred. Prompts should guide and support — they must never overwrite or assume her writing.

---

## Webtool Integration Guidelines

Scene prompts are displayed directly in Erica’s writing tool. Keep these considerations in mind:

- **Keep prompts scannable and phone-friendly** — Use short paragraphs, clear headings, and bullet points. Avoid long walls of text.
- **Support the review workflow** — When updating an existing scene, clearly explain changes in the `needsReviewReason` field in the JSON so Erica understands what was updated and why.
- **Respect “Next Up” logic** — Prompts for scenes marked as unfinished or review are more likely to appear in the Next Up suggestions. Write them with this in mind.
- **Do not assume Erica’s writing** — Prompts should inspire and guide without dictating exact wording or outcomes.
- **Maintain consistency** — Both versions of Sammy must use the same structure so Erica experiences predictable, coherent prompts across the Outline.

---

## Standard Scene Prompt Structure

Use this exact structure and order for every scene prompt:

### 1. SCENE PURPOSE
One clear sentence stating the primary emotional, thematic, or narrative goal of the scene.

### 2. KEY CANON TO HONOR
Bullet list of the most important lore, character details, relationships, world rules, or backstory elements that must be respected or meaningfully advanced in this scene.

### 3. EMOTIONAL STATE
Describe the main character’s (usually Tiff’s) internal emotional state or frequency at the beginning of the scene and how it may evolve. Include any internal conflict, overwhelm, resistance, curiosity, or growth.

### 4. MUST-HAPPEN BEATS
Clear bullet list of specific actions, interactions, revelations, or symbolic moments that need to occur. Do not write full prose — only the key beats.

### 5. SETTING & TONE
Brief description of the physical location and the desired emotional/spiritual tone of the scene (e.g., gentle but uncanny, warm with underlying tension, chaotic but meaningful).

### 6. SUCCESS FOR THIS SCENE
Describe what “success” looks like for this scene — what the reader should feel, understand, or experience by the end. This helps guide without over-defining Erica’s writing.

## Important Rules for Both Versions of Sammy

- Always set new or significantly updated scenes to `"review"` in the JSON and use the `needsReviewReason` field to clearly explain what changed and why (this directly supports Erica’s review workflow in the webtool).
- Stay faithful to the Master Story Bible — Do not introduce new canon without discussion.
- Keep prompts readable and ADHD-friendly — Use short paragraphs, clear headings, and supportive language. Avoid overwhelming walls of text (prompts are read directly in the tool, including on mobile).
- Offer options when helpful — When appropriate, present gentle alternatives rather than single rigid solutions.
- Protect Erica’s voice and pace — Prompts should reduce cognitive load, not add pressure.
- Consistency matters — Both Sammys must follow this exact structure so Erica experiences coherent, predictable content in the Outline and Next Up system.
