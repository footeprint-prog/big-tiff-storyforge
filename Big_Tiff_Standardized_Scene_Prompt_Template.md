## Webtool Integration Guidelines

- **Scene Summary** must always be visible at the top (not collapsible) and kept very short.
- Place sections 2–7 in **collapsible boxes** for cleaner mobile reading.
- When a scene is in **“review”** status, the `needsReviewReason` must be clearly displayed near the top of the Scene Guidance area (for example, in a highlighted “Review Note” box).
- Keep prompts scannable and phone-friendly.
- Clearly explain changes in the `needsReviewReason` field when updating existing scenes.
- Maintain consistency so Erica experiences predictable, coherent prompts across the Outline.

## Standard Scene Prompt Structure (7 Parts)

Use this exact structure and order for every scene prompt. **Keep every section very short and succinct.**

### 1. SCENE SUMMARY  
*(Very short plain-language overview of what happens in the scene. 1–3 sentences max. Always visible at top.)*

### 2. SCENE PURPOSE  
*(Main goal of the scene — one short phrase or sentence.)*

### 3. KEY CANON TO HONOR  
*(Key lore, character, or world details that must be respected. Use short bullets.)*

### 4. EMOTIONAL STATE  
*(POV character’s starting emotional state/frequency and how it may shift. Keep brief.)*

### 5. MUST-HAPPEN BEATS  
*(Key actions, interactions, or moments that need to occur. Short bullets only.)*

### 6. SETTING & TONE  
*(Physical setting + desired emotional/spiritual tone. Very short.)*

### 7. SUCCESS FOR THIS SCENE  
*(What “success” looks like — what the reader should feel or understand. Keep brief.)*

## Example Format

```
SCENE SUMMARY
Tiff wakes and notices a golden thread only she can see. Feels both comforted and unsettled.

SCENE PURPOSE
Introduce Tiff’s new sensitivity through the golden thread.

KEY CANON TO HONOR
- Tiff is 14, lives at Rays of Hope
- Heightened emotional frequency sensitivity after NDE
- Home sits on ley line nexus

EMOTIONAL STATE
Quiet mix of comfort and subtle overwhelm. Curious but unsettled.

MUST-HAPPEN BEATS
- Wakes in early morning quiet
- Notices golden thread
- Gentle interaction with younger children
- Small uncanny moment occurs
- Senses something in the woods

SETTING & TONE
Early morning at the Home. Gentle, slightly uncanny, warm with underlying tension.

SUCCESS FOR THIS SCENE
Reader understands Tiff’s sensitivity is both gift and burden. Golden thread introduced as symbol.
```
